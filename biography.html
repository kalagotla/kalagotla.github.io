Biography

Academic Journey

I am a recent PhD graduate in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Cincinnati, where I pursued advanced research in computational fluid dynamics and machine learning from 2017 to 2025. My academic journey began at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Dhanbad, where I earned my Bachelor of Technology in Mechanical Engineering in 2015.

After completing my undergraduate studies, I joined the University of Cincinnati to pursue my Master's degree in Aerospace Engineering, which I completed in 2018. During this time, I developed a strong foundation in fluid mechanics, numerical methods, and computational techniques that would later form the basis of my doctoral research.

Research Evolution

My research interests have evolved from traditional computational fluid dynamics to the cutting-edge intersection of machine learning and physics. I began my doctoral studies focusing on particle image velocimetry (PIV) and Lagrangian particle tracking, but quickly recognized the potential of artificial intelligence to revolutionize how we approach complex fluid dynamics problems.

This realization led me to develop several innovative tools and methodologies, including BICSNet (Bilateral Inertia-Correction Sparse Network), lptlib (Lagrangian Particle Tracking Library), and a comprehensive synthetic image generator. These contributions have not only advanced the field but have also been made available to the broader scientific community through open-source platforms.

Professional Development

Throughout my academic career, I have been actively involved in both research and teaching. I have served as a Teaching Assistant for multiple courses, including Aerodynamic Simulations and Biostatistics, where I developed educational modules and APIs to introduce complex concepts to undergraduate students. My experience also includes significant industry collaboration through my work with Procter & Gamble's Simulation Center.

I have been fortunate to mentor numerous graduate and undergraduate students, sharing my expertise in programming, fluid mechanics, and machine learning. This mentoring experience has reinforced my commitment to fostering the next generation of engineers and researchers.

Research Philosophy

My research philosophy centers on the belief that the most impactful scientific advances come from interdisciplinary collaboration and the integration of traditional methods with emerging technologies. I am passionate about developing physics-aware algorithms that maintain the fundamental principles of fluid dynamics while leveraging the power of modern machine learning techniques.

I believe in making research tools accessible to the broader community, which is why I have made significant efforts to publish my software packages on PyPI and maintain comprehensive documentation. This open-source approach not only accelerates scientific progress but also ensures that advanced computational tools are available to researchers worldwide.

Future Vision

Having recently completed my PhD, I am excited about the opportunities to continue advancing the field of computational fluid dynamics through innovative research and collaboration. I am particularly interested in exploring applications of physics-informed machine learning in aerospace engineering and contributing to the development of next-generation computational tools for complex multiphysics problems.

I am always eager to discuss potential collaborations, research opportunities, and innovative approaches to solving complex engineering challenges. My goal is to continue contributing to the scientific community while fostering an inclusive and collaborative research environment.
