About

I am a recent PhD graduate in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Cincinnati, specializing in computational fluid dynamics and physics-aware machine learning. My research focuses on developing innovative solutions for complex multiphysics problems in supersonic flows, with particular emphasis on particle image velocimetry (PIV) and advanced numerical methods.

Research Focus: My work bridges the gap between traditional computational fluid dynamics and cutting-edge deep learning techniques. I develop physics-informed neural networks and advanced particle tracking algorithms to solve challenging problems in fluid dynamics, particularly in supersonic and hypersonic flow regimes.

Key Contributions:

  • BICSNet (evolved from PIVnet): Developed a novel bilateral inertia-correction sparse network that achieves 76% error reduction in PIV data analysis for oblique shock flows
  • lptlib: Created a highly-parallelized Lagrangian particle tracking library with polydisperse simulation capabilities and Eulerian field conversion
  • Synthetic Image Generator: Built a comprehensive tool for generating synthetic PIV data with parallelization support for large-scale simulations
  • Publications: Contributing author to multiple peer-reviewed papers in AIAA conferences and journals

Technical Expertise:

  • Computational Methods: CFD, finite volume methods, particle tracking, numerical analysis
  • Machine Learning: Deep neural networks, physics-informed AI, computer vision, PyTorch, TensorFlow
  • Programming: Python, C++, MATLAB, parallel computing (OpenMP, MPI)
  • Research Tools: OpenFOAM, ANSYS Fluent, PIV analysis software, Git, LaTeX

Research Vision: I am passionate about advancing the field of computational fluid dynamics through the integration of machine learning and traditional numerical methods. My goal is to develop robust, physics-aware algorithms that can handle complex multiphysics problems while maintaining computational efficiency and accuracy. I believe in the power of interdisciplinary collaboration and am committed to making research tools accessible to the broader scientific community through open-source development.

Collaboration & Mentoring: I actively collaborate with researchers across multiple institutions and enjoy mentoring undergraduate and graduate students. I believe in fostering an inclusive research environment and am committed to advancing diversity in engineering and science. My collaborative approach has led to successful partnerships with industry and academic institutions.

I am always excited to discuss potential collaborations, research opportunities, and innovative approaches to solving complex engineering challenges. Feel free to reach out if you're interested in working together on cutting-edge research in computational fluid dynamics, machine learning, or aerospace engineering.
